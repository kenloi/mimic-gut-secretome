**[S1 · Title — "Listen to your gut"]**
Your gut carries a hundred times more microbial genes than human genes — / and most of what they do to us, we have never read. *(0:10)*

**[S2 · 100× more microbial genes — what do they do?]**
We carry a hundred times more microbial genes than our own, / and we already know microbes signal to us in *chemistry* — some molecules even *mimic* our own ligands. / *(click)* The metabolites are mapped. The secreted *proteins* are not — / do they signal too? *(0:18)*

**[S3 · A billion smORFs, filtered to one candidate — PROTECT]**
So we asked that with structure. / Through bioinformatic filtering, structure prediction, and ranking of candidates, / we found a microbial peptide that engages the **interleukin-7 receptor**. / One honest note along the way: our first hit scored the *precursor*; a human caught it, and with Claude Science we re-derived every *mature* peptide, refolded the shortlist in one session, and reranked — which surfaced this lead. *(0:26)*

**[S4 · A gut peptide lands on the IL-7 site — PROTECT · staged structure]**
Why does that matter? / **IL-7Rα** is the receptor for the cytokine that keeps our T cells alive — lose it and you lose adaptive immunity. / So watch what lands in its groove: / *(click)* the residues where immune-deficiency mutations cluster / *(click)* the natural cytokine, IL-7 / *(click)* and our gut peptide — the same surface. / Honestly: this is *co-location* with the functional interface, not enrichment. *(0:26)*

**[S5 · It picks IL-7Rα out of its own family — PROTECT]**
And it is picky. / We put it against the five receptors most like IL-7Rα — its own family — / and only the real target lights up; every look-alike stays dark. / AlphaFold3 agrees independently. Two engines, one answer. *(0:14)*

**[S6 · It rides a mobile element into a pathogen — PROTECT]**
And it's mobile. / The peptide rides a conjugative element across two hundred thirty-nine gut species — / enrichment p equals nought-point-nought-nought-three — / including fifteen strains of *Clostridioides difficile*, a colitis pathogen. *(0:20)*

**[S7 · A shield you can trade — name MIMIC + testable — PROTECT]**
Our leading hypothesis, one of two: a shield. / IL-7 keeps lymphocytes alive; muting it would dampen the immune response to whatever microbe carries the peptide — / a trait the mobile element can *trade*, commensal to pathogen. / We name it **MIMIC** — Mobile, ImmunoModulatory, ICE-borne, Commensal. / *(click)* Testable in a week: add MIMIC with IL-7, read STAT5 — / suppressed, it's a shielding *antagonist*; firing alone, an *agonist*. / A hypothesis, not yet function. *(0:28)*

**[S8 · Close — single takeaway]**
MIMIC is one peptide. / The gut may encode a whole *library* of human-receptor ligands — / waiting in microbial genomes to be read. *(0:12)*
